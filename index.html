What this is: The German Catholic Church operates as 27 legally independent dioceses — each a Körperschaft des öffentlichen Rechts (KdöR, a public-law corporation) — with no consolidated group accounts, no single balance sheet, and no obligation to produce one. This analysis constructs the first fully consolidated market-value balance sheet across all 27 dioceses and six additional structural layers (shadow entities, banks, pensions, housing, parishes, land). The result: a single system-level view of assets, liabilities, and equity that has never previously existed.

Key finding: At €134–155B in consolidated market-value total assets and €90–107B in equity, the system is comparable in scale to Germany's largest corporate groups — but with an equity ratio of 67–69%, zero financial debt, zero tax, and €6.74B in recurring Kirchensteuer (church tax — a compulsory income tax surcharge of 8–9% collected by the state on behalf of the church from all registered Catholics; €6.738B confirmed for 2023 per VDD consolidated accounts).

Critical caveat — realisability: This equity is not liquid, not tradeable, and has never been market-tested. There is no mechanism to sell, distribute, or leverage these assets at system level. No shares exist. No market exists. The €90–107B NAV is an analytical construct — it measures what the system owns net of obligations, not what could be realised. Individual dioceses can and do sell property, but system-level liquidity is effectively zero.
Glossary: KdöR = Körperschaft des öffentlichen Rechts (public-law corporation — a unique German legal form granting sovereign-like privileges including tax exemption, self-governance, and the right to levy taxes) · Kirchensteuer / KiSt = Church tax (8–9% surcharge on income tax, collected by the Finanzamt and remitted to the dioceses; €6.738B system-wide in 2023 per VDD) · HGB = Handelsgesetzbuch (German Commercial Code — the accounting standard used by all dioceses; cost-basis, no fair-value option) · Erbbaurecht = Heritable building right (a 75–99 year ground lease where the church retains land ownership and receives an annual Erbbauzins of ~4% of land value) · Kirchenstiftung = Parish foundation (the legal entity for each parish — ~9,291 nationally, each an independent KdöR)

How church pensions work: The system has three distinct pension layers. KZVK (Layer A) is a funded pension asset pool — €34.1B Bilanzsumme (2023 audited GB), of which €26.6B in Kapitalanlagen, backing lay employee pensions across 3 Abrechnungsverbände (F=Tarif 2002, G=Tarif 2016, P=Tarif 2022). Insurance-technical provisions: €34.0B. It is mostly eliminated on consolidation. Emeritenanstalt (Layer B) entities hold €1.65B+ in assets to fund priest pensions — but these are severely underfunded (many with equity of just €1). On-BS Provisions (Layer C) are the €7.2B of pension liabilities sitting directly on diocesan HGB balance sheets (v2.4: Paderborn confirmed €553M, Köln confirmed €823M — both materially above prior estimates). On a buyout basis (IAS 19 + insurance premium), the total pension obligation across all three layers is estimated at €17–21B — a €10–14B gap vs. the €7.2B booked under HGB. The KZVK disclosed total underfunding of €6.9B across all participating employers (per Paderborn 2024 FB, citing KZVK 31.12.2024).

Measurement Approach

We use IFRS 10 consolidation logic to define the perimeter (all 7 layers) but measure everything at estimated market value. IFRS fair value (IAS 40, IAS 19) is an accounting construct that serves auditors. For an institutional audience, we go straight to market value. The result: one consolidated MV balance sheet. No HGB column, no IFRS column.

Securities MTM (MKT_FA = 1.29×)

Weighted average of 9 auditor Anhang disclosures. Range 1.03–1.45×. Paderborn 1.33×, Köln 1.33×, Fulda 1.19–1.45×, Bamberg 1.31×, Würzburg 1.03–1.06×, Aachen 1.06×.

Property Revaluation (MKT_SA = 2.5× blended)

v2.0: Split approach. Sakralgebäude (cathedrals, ~200 at diocesan level) at 0× (cultural heritage). All other diocesan property (offices, residential, commercial) at ~2.78× → blended ~2.5×. Source: Bamberg Verkehrswert, cost-basis dioceses 2.0–3.5× uplift. Does NOT include Layer 7 land.

Pension IAS 19 → Buyout (1.3× then +15–35%)

HGB discount ~1.9%. IAS 19 uses iBoxx AA curve (~3.2%). Buyout adds 15–35% over IAS 19 DBO (Willis Towers Watson benchmarks). Three layers: KZVK deficit €3.5–4.5B (total underfunding €6.9B per 2024 disclosure), Emeritenanstalt €3.0–4.0B under IAS 19, on-BS provisions €7.2B remeasured (v2.4: sum of 26/27 diocesan provisions; Paderborn €553M, Köln €823M confirmed from 2024 FBs).

Land Valuation (v2.1: ~240K+ ha, excl. church footprint)

v2.1 (Mar 2026): Church footprint zeroed. BORIS-revised: 5-diocese weighted BRW €250/m² (was €150). Thünen 2024: 381K ha combined church agricultural → Catholic est. 150K+ ha (was 115K). Freiburg confirmed 4% Erbbauzins rate. Revised: Urban €30.3B, Agri €4.5B, Forest €1.3B, Erbbau €12B, footprint -€5.2B. Layer 7 mid: €42.9B (was €30.5B). 45,740 Erbbaurecht contracts now confirmed (46% of est. 100K).

Parish Triangulation (v2.1: churches at €0, München finding)

v2.1: 16,700 church buildings = €0 (cultural heritage). Non-church property €9.5B (BRW uplift, Kita 35% on-BS offset). Financial assets €5.2B (München 750 Kirchenstiftungen ~€2B off-BS discovery). Coverage improved 12%→31.5% of parishes (2,922 of 9,291). Kita carrier split: 30-40% still on parish BS, balance in gGmbH structures. Consensus: €14.7B ±€3.5B.

Intercompany Eliminations

~€18B diocesan deposits at church banks (est.; constrained by bank TA €28.7B less €8.9B 3rd-party deposits less ~€1.5B bank equity). €34.1B KZVK overlap (Bilanzsumme double-counted with diocesan pension provisions). €2–5B other interco (housing subsidiary equity, cross-entity receivables). Total: €56–59B eliminated.

Environmental / Heritage (New Line)

24,000+ listed buildings × conservation mandates. Asbestos/remediation liabilities. Deferred maintenance backlog. Range €3–8B. No HGB equivalent — this liability does not appear in any published account.
